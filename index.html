Welcome!

My name is Aniket Sanghi (he/him/his) and I am an astronomer-in-training and sophomore at the University of Texas at Austin. Self-driven, productive, and a meticulous planner, I am driven by my passion for Physics, Astronomy, Technology, and Scientific Communication. I seek to earn a Ph.D. in Astronomy and aim to to lead the scientific priorities of the next generation of telescopes by designing, proposing, and conducting large-scale, high-impact research programs and surveys, to push the boundaries of science that the astronomical community can accomplish. I strongly value creativity, building meaningful connections, and the role of inclusive mentorship in personal growth.

I am actively involved in the Department of Astronomy at UT Austin through my participation in three research projects. First, I am working with Dr. Yifan Zhou and Dr. Brendan Bowler on reference star differential imaging of the PDS70 planetary system using HST/WFC3 images. Second, I am working with Dr. William M.J. Best on constraining the initial mass function (IMF) of low-mass stars and brown dwarfs. Third, I am working with Dr. Michael H. Montgomery and Zachary Vanderbosch to identify and analyze variable stars and eclipsing systems in archival LCO photometric data. These projects are helping me develop a broad understanding of different sub-fields in astronomy to be able to apply a multidisciplinary approach towards defining the scientific goals of my future research programs.
